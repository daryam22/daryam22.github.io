Elephants: Gentle Giants

Despite being the largest land mammals, elephants are not ferocious nor beast-like. Actually, they epitomize the term "gentle giant" as they are intelligent and social creatures that occupy very similar traits to humans. Here is a link to an interesting elephant doing a well known human activity. Painter

Gentle

Elephants have an increased sensibility and an increased sense of family. Female elephants tend to spend their entire lives in one herd and they tend to be extremely associated with others in the herd as if they are siblings, parents, and even grandparents. Here is a link to an example of the close-knit elephant social structure. Fallen

Giants

Despite their gentle nature, elephants are physically the largest land mammals. Even at birth, calves weigh between 200-250 pounds. Adult elephants weigh between 4,000 and 14,000 pounds. Females are slightly smaller weighing between 6,000-8,000 pounds. Here is an image that truly illustrates the magnificent stature of an elephant. Giants
